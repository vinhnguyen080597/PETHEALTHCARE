# Pet Health Care - Facebook Content Plan

This file is the working context for Facebook Page communication before release.
Use it to continue writing daily posts without losing the brand direction, sequence, or completed post history.

## Page Basics

- Page name: Pet Health Care
- Main audience: Vietnamese pet owners, especially dog/cat owners ("Sen")
- Posting rhythm: 1 post per day
- Tone: cute, warm, caring, easy to understand
- Language: Vietnamese-first
- Main character/persona: Mai, a friendly pet-care assistant
- Current phase: pre-release communication, not hard selling

## Brand Positioning

Pet Health Care is a pet-care companion that helps Sen observe, record, and care for pets more proactively every day.

The product should not be positioned as an app that "diagnoses disease". Use safer wording:

- AI-assisted early screening
- Initial health signal check
- Helps Sen observe and organize information
- Does not replace veterinarians
- Encourages Sen to visit a vet when red flags appear

## Core Message

Short version:

> Pet Health Care helps Sen care for each pet more proactively by keeping health records, reminders, AI-assisted screening, and care history in one place.

Vietnamese direction:

> Pet Health Care đồng hành cùng Sen trong việc quan sát, ghi nhớ và chăm sóc bé cưng chủ động hơn mỗi ngày.

## Communication Pillars

### 1. Education For Sen

Simple, practical care knowledge:

- Warning signs in dogs/cats
- Vaccine and deworming basics
- Weight, appetite, stool, skin, eyes, ears
- When to monitor at home vs when to visit a vet
- How to prepare information before going to the vet

### 2. Daily Pain Points

Content based on common pet-owner worries:

- "Bé hơi lạ, không biết có sao không"
- "Quên lịch tiêm"
- "Không nhớ lần trước bé bị gì"
- "Ảnh, giấy tờ, lịch khám nằm lung tung"
- "Không biết nên mô tả triệu chứng thế nào cho bác sĩ"

### 3. Product Teasers

Softly introduce features before release:

- Pet profile
- Care Passport / care record
- Vaccine and reminder records
- AI health screening
- Breed recognition
- Vet-ready summary
- Pet Feed for structured breeder listings

### 4. Mai Companion

Use Mai as a warm assistant voice:

- "Mai nhắc Sen..."
- "Mai cùng Sen để ý..."
- "Mai không thay bác sĩ thú y, nhưng Mai giúp Sen ghi nhớ tốt hơn..."

### 5. Responsible AI

Build trust by being careful:

- AI only supports initial screening
- Results are reference only
- Severe signs should be checked by a veterinarian
- Sen should not self-medicate based only on online/app content

## Standard Post Format

For most posts:

1. Hook: one short emotional or practical opening.
2. Context: describe a familiar pet-owner situation.
3. Value: 3-5 useful points or one clear lesson.
4. Home-care guidance: what Sen can safely do at home when signs are still mild, focused on comfort, observation, hydration, hygiene, and note-taking.
5. Pet Health Care bridge: softly connect to the app vision.
6. Safety line: mention vet care if relevant.
7. CTA: ask Sen to follow/comment/share/save.
8. Hashtags.

## Caption Styling Rules

Each Facebook post should be visually easy to scan, not plain text only.

Use:

- Emojis/icons lightly in headings or section starters, for example: 🐾, 🐶, 🐱, 💛, 📌, ✅, ⚠️, 🩺, 📅, 📝, ✨
- Facebook-ready emphasis for key hooks, section labels, and important takeaways.
- Do not leave Markdown syntax such as `**bold**` or `*italic*` inside final captions because Facebook will show the asterisks as plain text.
- Prefer uppercase headings, clear emoji section markers, and short standout lines for Facebook readability.
- Short paragraphs, usually 1-3 lines each.
- Bullet points when listing signs, tips, or checklist items.
- Plenty of spacing between sections.

Avoid:

- Too many emojis in one paragraph.
- Overusing bold on every line.
- Long blocks of text without line breaks.
- Alarmist medical icons or scary visual language unless the topic is genuinely about red flags.

Suggested caption shape:

```text
🐾 MAIN HOOK

Short emotional intro.

📌 SECTION LABEL
- Point 1
- Point 2
- Point 3

🏠 SEN CÓ THỂ LÀM GÌ TẠI NHÀ KHI DẤU HIỆU CÒN NHẸ?
- Safe care/comfort step 1
- Observation/note-taking step 2
- What not to self-medicate or force

Mai nhắc nhẹ Sen nè: gentle reminder.

✅ CTA line.

#PetHealthCare #ChamSocThuCung
```
